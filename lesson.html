Angles/Lines

  • point: a specific location in space
  • plane: a collection of all points extending without end in two directions
  • line: a straight path of points that extends without end in two directions
  • parallel lines: lines that lie in the same place with no points in common
  • perpendicular lines: lines that intersect at right angles (90 degrees)
  • ray: a part of a line that begins at a point and extends without end in one direction
  • angle: formed by two rays that originate at the same point
  • vertex: the common endpoint of an angle
  • acute angle: an angle that is less than 90 degrees
  • obtuse angle: an angle that is greater than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees
  • right angle: an angle that is 90 degrees
  • line segment: a part of a line that lies between two endpoints
  • complementary angles: two angles with the sum of their angles equal to 90 degrees
  • straight angles: an angle that measures 180 degrees
  • linear pair: two adjacent angles that form a straight line or straight angle
  • supplementary angles: two angles with the sum of their angles equal to 180 degrees
  • vertical angles: opposite angles formed at the intersection of two lines, they are congruent
  • transversal: a line that intersects two or more lines
  • corresponding angles: a pair of angles in matching positions, they are congruent when formed by a transversal that intersects two parallel lines
  • alternate interior angles: angles between parallel lines but on opposite sides of the transversal, they are congruent
  • alternate exterior angles: angles outside parallel lines but on opposite sides of the transveral, they are congruent

Triangles

  • Classified by sides
    • scalene triangle: a triangle with no congruent sides
    • isosceles triangle: a triangle with at least two congruent sides
    • equilateral triangle: a triangle with all congruent sides
  • Classified by angles
    • acute triangle: a triangle with all angles acute
    • obtuse triangle: a triangle with one obtuse angle
    • right triangle: a triangle with one right angle
  • similar triangles: triangles that have the same shape but different sides, corresponding angles are congruent and corresponding sides are proportionate
  • hypotenuse: the side opposite the right angle in a right triangle
  • legs: the two sides of a right triangle tha form a right angle
